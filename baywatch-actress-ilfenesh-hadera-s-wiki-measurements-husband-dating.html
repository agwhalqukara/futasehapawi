Who is Ilfenesh Hadera?

Ilfenesh Hadera was born on the 1st December 1985, in New York USA, and is an actress, best known to the world as Stephanie Holden in the film “Baywatch”, and as Deb Kawi in the TV series “Billions”, among other roles she has secured so far in her career. Do you want to know more about this rising star, from her birth to the present? We will cover all the important details about Hadera’s life and career, so stay with us, you won’t regret it.

Ilfenesh Hadera Childhood, Parents, and Education

Let’s start with Ilfenesh’s early years; she was born to an Ethiopian father, a refugee named Asfaha Hader, who later founded the African Services Committee, focused on improving the lives of African immigrants to the US and a European mother, Kim Nicholas, who worked as an acupuncturist. She matriculated from the Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music & Art and Performing Arts, then furthered her studies at Harlem School of Arts, but didn’t pursue acting as a profession until later in life, as she first helped her father at the African Services Committee, and also worked as a waitress.

Career Start

Frightened by the possibility of failure, Ilfenesh delayed her acting debut until 2010, when she secured the role of Hazel in the film “comedy-drama film “1/20”, starring Pete Aka Bmske, Patrick Aleph and Humbert Altaf. The following year she appeared in the television film “Da Brick”, while in 2013 she was Judy in the action drama film “Oldboy”, starring Josh Brolin, Elizabeth Olson and Samuel L. Jackson.

Rise to Prominence

Hadera’s career improved steadily – she became more recognized in the film industry, and in 2015 she was chosen for her first recurring role, as Carmen Febles in the TV mini-series “Show Me a Hero”, then the same year also appeared in the film Chi-Raq” in the role of Ms. McCloud, which was enough to secure her the role of Deb Kawi in the TV series “Billions”, which remains one of her most prominent roles so far. She played Deb in 20 episodes of the award-nominated drama series, which launched her to stardom. She continued successfully, first in 2017 with the role of Stephanie Holden in the box office film hit “Baywatch”, starring next to Dwayne Johnson and Alexandra Daddario, and then with the part of Opal Gilstrap in the TV comedy series “She’s Gotta Have It”.

“Deception” and Further Projects

After that role, Ilfenesh was chosen for another television role, this time as Kay Daniels in the TV drama series “Deception”, starring next to Jack Cutmore-Scott and Lenora Crichlow, which premiered on the 11th March 2018 on ABC, but it was canceled following the last episode of season one. Nevertheless, Ilfenesh soon found a new engagement, as Mayme Johnson in the new TV series “The Godfather of Harlem”, which is now filming in late 2018.
