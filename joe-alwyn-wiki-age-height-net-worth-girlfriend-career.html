Joe Alwyn Biography

Joe Alwyn was born on February 21, 1991, as name Joseph Matthew Alwyn, in Tunbridge Well, Kent, England, United Kingdom. He is currently 32 years old. His zodiac sign is Pisces. He is a son to father, Richard Alwyn who is a documentary filmmaker, and to mother Elizabeth Alwyn who is a psychotherapist. He is the great-grandson of composer William Alwyn.

Joe Alwyn Height And Weight

Joe Alwyn is British by nationality and is of the white race. Joe is also 6 feet 1 inch (1.85 m) tall and weighs approximately 86 kg (190 lbs). In addition, his hair is blonde, and his eyes are blue.

Joe Alwyn’s Childhood

Alwyn went to the City of London School and graduated from there. In his late teens, he also joined the National Youth Theatre. While studying literature and drama at the University of Bristol, he appeared in two student productions at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival. He earned a BA in acting from the Royal Central School of Speech and Drama after graduating in 2012.

Career

Within two weeks of his graduate showcase in early 2015, he began his acting career. In 2016, he played the lead in Ang Lee’s war drama “Billy Lynn’s Long Halftime Walk,” based on Ben Foundation’s novel. The following year, he appeared in the English drama film “The Sense of an Ending” as a supporting actor. In 2018, he starred in Yorgos Lanthimos’ award-winning period drama “The Favourite.” Later that year, he appeared in the films “Operation Finale,” “Boy Erased,” and “Mary Queen of Scots.” While “Boy Erased” is a biographical drama film about the son of Baptist parents who is forced to participate in gay conversion therapy. As a result, “Mary Queen of Scots” is a historical drama film based on the life of Mary I of Scotland, who ruled over Scotland from 1542 to 1567. He did, however, receive the Chopard Trophee alongside Elizabeth Debicki at the Cannes Film Festival. In 2019, Alwyn co-starred as slave owner Gideon Brodess in the award-winning biographical drama “Harriet,” about abolitionist Harriet Tubman. On September 10, 2019, it had its world premiere at the Toronto International Film Festival. Later that year, in August, he joined the cast of “The Souvenir Part II,” and in December, he appeared as Bob Cratchit in the BBC/FX three-part miniseries “A Christmas Carol.” Joe also appeared in the film adaptation of Jojo Moyes’ novel “The Last Letter from Your Lover.”
In addition, he co-starred in the Emily Bronte biopic “Emily” with Emma Mackey.
